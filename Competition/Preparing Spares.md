# Preparing Spares

## Process

1. A mentor examines each mechanism individually and independently, writing down every part they think could break or need replacement.
2. The mentor calls over a student and asks them what they think could break or may need a replacement later.
3. The combined input is used to create a final list of spares to make or purchase.

## Rules for Purchased Components

For purchased components, the general rule is to have **1 spare for each unit used**.

> Example: If the robot uses 4 × 12-tooth pulleys, keep 4 × 12-tooth pulleys in the spare box.

## Types of Spares

### CNC Spares

Parts that can be fully produced on the CNC machine without any additional processing.

### Hand-Fabricated Spares

Parts that are made entirely by hand (e.g., cut, drilled, filed by hand without CNC).

### CNC Spares with Hand Fabrication

Parts that are CNC-machined but also require additional hand work such as counter-sinking holes.

### Completed Spare Mechanisms

Full sub-assemblies or mechanisms that are built and ready to swap onto the robot if needed.
