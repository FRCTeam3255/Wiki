# Motor RPM

This page displays the maximum RPM (Revolutions Per Minute) of common motors used in FRC robotics.

!!! note

    These values represent the free speed (no load) RPM for each motor.

## Motor Specifications

| Motor         | Max RPM (Free Speed) | Stall Torque | Stall Current | Free Current | Power Output | Weight   |
| ------------- | -------------------- | ------------ | ------------- | ------------ | ------------ | -------- |
| Kraken x60    | 6,000 RPM            | 7.09 N·m     | 366 A         | 2 A          | 641 W        | 1.1 lbs  |
| Kraken x44    | 5,800 RPM            | 5.84 N·m     | 366 A         | 2 A          | 528 W        | 0.77 lbs |
| Falcon 500    | 6,380 RPM            | 4.69 N·m     | 257 A         | 1.5 A        | 783 W        | 1.1 lbs  |
| CIM           | 5,330 RPM            | 2.41 N·m     | 131 A         | 2.7 A        | 337 W        | 2.8 lbs  |
| Mini CIM      | 5,840 RPM            | 1.41 N·m     | 89 A          | 3 A          | 215 W        | 2.16 lbs |
| Bag Motor     | 13,180 RPM           | 0.43 N·m     | 53 A          | 1.8 A        | 149 W        | 0.71 lbs |

## Additional Details

### Modern Motors (Brushless)

- **Kraken x60**: High-performance brushless motor with integrated FOC motor controller
- **Kraken x44**: Compact brushless motor with integrated FOC motor controller
- **Falcon 500**: Brushless motor with integrated Talon FX controller

### Traditional Motors (Brushed)

- **CIM**: Classic workhorse motor, widely used in FRC
- **Mini CIM**: Smaller version of the CIM motor
- **Bag Motor**: Lightweight, high-speed motor for auxiliary mechanisms

!!! tip

    When selecting a motor, consider not just the RPM but also the torque requirements, weight constraints, and available space on your robot.
